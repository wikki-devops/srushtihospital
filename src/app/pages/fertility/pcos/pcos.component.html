Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a common hormonal disorder affecting women of reproductive age, characterized by an excess production of androgens (male hormones). This imbalance leads to symptoms such as irregular menstrual cycles, infertility, acne, weight gain, and excessive hair growth.

A significant concern with PCOS is its impact on fertility, as it often causes anovulation, where eggs are not released, accounting for around 70% of infertility cases linked to this condition. Additionally, women with PCOS are at a higher risk of developing health issues, with a 50% increased likelihood of developing type 2 diabetes before age 40 due to insulin resistance. Managing PCOS is crucial to minimize these long-term health risks.
