Uterine Polyps

What Are Polyps?

Polyps are soft overgrowths of the inner lining of the uterus (endometrium). They’re like tiny obstacles that sometimes prevent implantation or cause bleeding between periods.

Frequently Asked Questions About Uterine Polyps

Do polyps always need removal?

If polyps are causing symptoms or infertility, removal is recommended for better outcomes.

Is it painful?

No. Polyp removal via hysteroscopy is a gentle, minimally invasive procedure with minimal discomfort.

Can it return?

Rarely. While recurrence is uncommon, regular follow-ups are advised.

Can it turn cancerous?

Extremely rare. Most uterine polyps are benign and non-cancerous.

Will it cause miscarriage?

Possibly. If left untreated, uterine polyps may interfere with implantation or lead to pregnancy loss.

Can I delay treatment?

Best not to, especially if you are trying to conceive. Timely removal improves fertility outcomes.

Will periods normalize?

Often, yes. Many women notice lighter, more regular periods after polyp removal.

Can it be diagnosed in ultrasound?

Yes. A transvaginal ultrasound or saline sonography can help detect uterine polyps effectively.

Is hysteroscopy safe?

Very. Hysteroscopy is a commonly performed, safe outpatient procedure used for both diagnosis and removal.

Can I conceive right after?

Yes. In many cases, fertility improves immediately after polyp removal and conception happens soon after.
