Ovarian Cysts

What Are Ovarian Cysts?

Ovarian cysts are fluid-filled sacs that form on or inside the ovaries. Most are harmless and go away on their own — but some can affect fertility or cause discomfort. Understanding them early can save you years of confusion and worry.

Stats in India

Nearly 1 in 10 Indian women are found to have ovarian cysts during reproductive years. Most go unnoticed unless they interfere with periods or fertility.

Treatment

Some cysts shrink naturally. For others, we may prescribe hormonal treatment or gently remove them via laparoscopy — preserving the ovary and protecting fertility.

When IVF Helps

When cysts are multiple or hormonal (like in PCOS), or if they damage the ovary, IVF may be the better route. It gives control over eggs and boosts pregnancy chances.

Frequently Asked Questions About Ovarian Cysts

Will all cysts need surgery?

No. Many cysts, especially functional ones, dissolve on their own without any surgical treatment.

Do cysts always cause symptoms?

Not always. Many cysts are silent and discovered only during routine scans.

Can cysts return?

Sometimes, yes. Recurrence depends on the type of cyst and underlying hormonal conditions.

Will I lose my ovary?

Very rarely. Most cysts can be removed while preserving the ovary.

Is pregnancy possible with cysts?

Yes, often. Many women conceive with cysts, especially if they’re monitored and managed appropriately.

Can cysts cause cancer?

Very rarely. Most cysts are benign, but complex or persistent ones may need evaluation to rule out malignancy.

What’s the pain like?

It varies — some feel a dull ache, others sharp or sudden pain, especially if a cyst bursts or twists.

Will it affect egg count?

Only some cysts, like endometriomas, can affect ovarian reserve. Most cysts do not impact egg count significantly.

Is it linked to PCOS?

Not always. PCOS is a hormonal condition involving multiple small follicles, not the same as a single ovarian cyst.

Can stress worsen it?

Indirectly, yes. Stress affects hormonal balance, which can contribute to irregular cycles and possibly cyst formation.
