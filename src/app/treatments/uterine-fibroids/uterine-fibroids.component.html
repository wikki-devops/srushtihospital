Uterine Fibroids

What Are Fibroids?

Fibroids are benign (non-cancerous) muscle growths in the uterus. They’re like uninvited guests — often harmless, but when large or poorly placed, they can impact your fertility or pregnancy.

Frequently asked questions

Are fibroids cancerous?

No, fibroids are non-cancerous (benign) growths in the uterus. They do not turn into cancer.

Will I need surgery?

Surgery is only needed if fibroids are affecting your fertility, causing severe symptoms, or not responding to other treatments.

Can fibroids grow back?

Yes, fibroids can grow back after treatment, but not in every case. The chances vary based on age, treatment type, and hormonal factors.

Do they cause miscarriage?

In some cases, large or poorly positioned fibroids can increase the risk of miscarriage — but many women with fibroids have healthy pregnancies.

Is myomectomy safe?

Yes, myomectomy — the surgical removal of fibroids — is a safe and effective procedure when performed by an experienced specialist.

Can I get pregnant with fibroids?

Absolutely. Many women with fibroids conceive naturally and go on to have healthy pregnancies.

Are fibroids painful?

They can be. Some women experience heavy periods, pelvic pain, or pressure, while others have no symptoms at all.

Is medicine alone enough?

Sometimes, yes. Medications can help manage symptoms like bleeding or pain, but may not eliminate fibroids completely.

Can it shrink after menopause?

Yes, fibroids often shrink after menopause due to a natural drop in hormone levels like estrogen and progesterone.

Can I deliver normally?

In many cases, yes. If fibroids aren’t blocking the birth canal or causing complications, a normal vaginal delivery is possible.
