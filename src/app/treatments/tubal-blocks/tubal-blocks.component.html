What is PCOS?

Your fallopian tubes are delicate pathways that carry the egg from the ovary to the uterus. When these tubes are blocked due to scarring, infection, or other conditions, the egg and sperm can't meet — making it difficult to conceive naturally. It’s a silent condition, but with the right care, it doesn’t have to be the end of your dream to become a parent.

How to Know if I Have a Tubal Block — A Real Conversation with Dr. Samundi Sankari

Patient: Doctor, I’ve been trying to get pregnant for two years. My periods are regular, but still no success. Could something be blocking it?
Dr. Samundi: Sometimes, the reason lies in the fallopian tubes. They may be blocked, not allowing the egg and sperm to meet. We usually check this through a simple imaging test called an HSG or with laparoscopy. Don’t worry — it’s more common than you think, and we have ways to help.
Patient : Is it treatable?
Dr. Samundi: Absolutely. In many cases, we can clear the blocks through microsurgery. And in others, IVF gives a beautiful chance for pregnancy. You’re not alone in this.

Statistics of Tubal Blocks Among Indian Married Women

Did you know? Over 20–25% of infertility cases among Indian women are linked to tubal blocks. Many of these women had no symptoms — only discovering it when they began trying to conceive. This makes timely diagnosis and gentle support all the more important.

Why IVF Was Chosen Instead of Surgery for Some

For some women, the damage to the tubes is too severe or the location of the block isn’t ideal for surgery. In these cases, IVF gives a safer, faster route to pregnancy. It bypasses the fallopian tubes altogether and brings the embryo directly into the womb — giving women the joy of motherhood without delay.

Frequently asked questions

Do tubal blocks cause pain?

Not always. Most women don’t feel any symptoms.

How is it diagnosed?

Through HSG (X-ray test) or laparoscopy.

Can I still get pregnant naturally?

Yes, if only one tube is open or after successful surgery.

Will I need IVF immediately?

Not always. We explore all options based on your condition.

Is microsurgery painful?

You It’s a minimally invasive procedure, with a short recovery time.

Is IVF safe for me?

Yes, IVF is a safe and proven option, especially for tubal factor infertility.

Can a past infection cause tubal blocks?

Yes, infections like tuberculosis or STDs can cause scarring.

Will age affect my chances?

Yes. The earlier we detect and treat, the better the success.

How long does IVF take?

About 2–3 weeks per cycle, including monitoring and embryo transfer.

Is this the end of natural pregnancy for me?

Not at all. Many women go on to conceive naturally after surgery, and others through IVF — both are beautiful journeys.
